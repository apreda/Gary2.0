The Home Page — 8 directions

Same brand voice on all eight (GARY A.I. wordmark, mono kickers, mono digits, gold, CASHED/LOST) and same real data (Sun Jul 5: France −1.5 LOST last night, MIN ML CASHED, 17 games today, first kickoff Norway @ Brazil 4:00 PM) — so you're comparing what the page fundamentally is, not styling. A comparison table + my read is at the bottom.
A · GARY'S DESK

Check on Gary — the ride-along

Home is Gary, present tense. The page opens on his state right now: how last night went (and when he wins, the page celebrates — gold wash, swagger; when he loses he owns it in one line), what he's doing next (countdown to his next call), what he's sweating live. You open the app the way you'd check on a friend who bets.

Why it wins
  • Nobody can copy it — it needs a character with a public ledger.
  • "When Gary wins we all win" becomes the literal structure of the page.
  • Never empty: mornings are "between nights" — result up top, anticipation below.
Watch out: needs disciplined first-person copy all day (generated from real events only — no canned filler).

B · THE RUNDOWN

The bettor's day-sheet

Home is the day itself: one continuous spine from last night to midnight. Settled games sink to the top with Gary's stamps, live games glow in the middle, tonight's slate queues below with times and his calls as they post. Scores + picks + results merged into a single scannable column — your three morning questions are literally the first three zones.

Why it wins
  • Highest daily utility per pixel — the page IS "what did I miss / what's live / what's on."
  • One mental model all day; nothing re-arranges, the day just flows down the sheet.
Watch out: character-light — Gary becomes marks on a schedule unless his voice rides the rows.

C · MISSION CONTROL

Tiles and doors

Home as the fastest possible orientation: a compact grid of live tiles (record, next event, live count, free pick) and big doors to the tabs that own depth. Ten-second read, zero scroll.

Why it wins
  • Clearest for day-0 users; nothing to learn.
  • Cheapest to keep truthful — tiles are just live numbers.
Watch out: it's the most generic direction — any app could ship this. Hard to love, impossible to screenshot.

D · THE TICKET

You're holding Gary's betslip

The most betting-native metaphor there is: Home IS Gary's ticket for today. His card renders as a physical slip — perforated edge, stake and odds per leg, stamps landing as games settle, a running day total at the bottom. Yesterday's settled ticket sits folded above it. "Riding with Gary" becomes literal: his ticket is in your hand.

Why it wins
  • Instantly legible to any bettor — everyone knows how to read a slip.
  • The object is shareable/screenshot-able by design (it already looks like the share card).
  • The day has a natural arc: ticket fills → stamps land → total settles.
Watch out: overlaps the Picks/Winners mental model — needs a crisp rule for what's on the Home ticket (e.g. the day's marquee card) vs what lives in Winners.

E · THE BROADCAST

Always on air

Home as a live show with Gary as anchor: a scores ticker crawls up top, an ON NOW panel carries whatever this second is about (the sweat during games, the countdown show pre-game, the morning-after desk at 9 AM), and an UP NEXT rundown queues the day like a control-room board. The page always feels ON AIR.

Why it wins
  • Maximum energy — the "fast-paced, dynamic" feel you asked for, structurally.
  • ON NOW gives every hour of the day a hero without re-architecting the page.
Watch out: tickers and pulsing LIVE chrome can tip into noise — needs restraint to stay premium, and motion costs battery/attention.

F · THE FEED

Following Gary, inside the app

Home is Gary's own feed — timestamped first-person posts generated from real events all day: the 7 AM grading post, the lineup note, each call as it posts (with the pick card embedded), sweat updates as games swing, the sign-off. Like following him on X, except richer and in-app. Infinite scroll back through his whole life.

Why it wins
  • The most character-alive direction — Gary literally talks to you all day.
  • Every event already exists in the backend; the feed is a presentation of real data, and it mirrors the @BetwithGary voice users may already follow.
  • Habit-forming shape users already understand (a timeline).
Watch out: voice volume — needs hard grounding rules (posts only from real events, no filler) and it buries "what's on tonight" unless a slate module rides above the stream.

G · THE BOARD

The whole day on one wall

Home as the big board — every game today as a compact tile on a grid wall, auto-sorted by state: live tiles glow first, upcoming queue by time, settled tiles dim with their stamps. Gary's side is lit gold on every tile. One glance = the entire day and exactly where Gary stands on it. Sportsbook lounge wall meets Bloomberg terminal, in our black and gold.

Why it wins
  • The strongest visual identity of the eight — a screenshot IS the brand.
  • Perfect for "I wonder who Gary has in this one" — find the tile, see the gold.
  • Density = the fast-paced feel without any motion tricks.
Watch out: tiles are tight — long team names, doubleheaders, and accessibility sizes need real design work; storytelling (recaps, celebration) needs a home above the wall.

H · TODAY'S EDITION

One cover, three times a day

The anti-dashboard: Home is a single bold cover that changes with the day — the morning-after cover (the night's story + the record), the afternoon cover (the marquee game + Gary's angle), the night cover (the sweat, huge). Massive type, one story, a contents row to everything else. Premium brand statement over utility.

Why it wins
  • The most striking single screen — pure brand, feels like a flagship app.
  • Celebration is built in: a winning morning IS the cover.
Watch out: utility lives one tap down — power users may find it slow by week two. Highest risk of "beautiful but why."

My read. These compose more than they compete. The strongest identities are the ones only Gary can wear: A (Desk), D (Ticket), and F (Feed) — all three are the character; B and G are the best utility spines; H is the best moment; C and E are grammars, not identities.

If I had to ship one: A's identity, D's hero object, G's middle. Concretely: the page opens as Gary's Desk (the celebration/ownership header + next-call countdown), the hero object is his Ticket for today (the slip filling with calls and stamps — the "ride with Gary" made physical), and below it the Board wall gives the whole day at a glance for the "who's Gary got in this one?" fan. H's cover treatment becomes the morning celebration state of the header rather than a whole page. F is the boldest future — Gary talking all day is the deepest moat — but it deserves its own bet (maybe as the center tab someday) rather than being Home's v1.

Tell me which of these feel like Gary — or which pieces from different ones — and that's what I'll build for real.
