M1 · HERO + TICKER RIBBON

Big hero, schedule underneath

The hero owns the full width — score huge, pick line clean. The other big games live in ONE slim horizontal ribbon pinned to the card's bottom edge, reading like a broadcast schedule bar. Swipes sideways if the day is long.

Why: one thing leads (the sweat), the day stays one glance, the card gets SHORTER not taller. My recommendation.

Ribbon chips are small — statuses must stay micro (▶2 · 4:00 · ✓).

M2 · THE FILMSTRIP

Swipe through the big games

Every big game is its own full hero card on a horizontal pager — the current one front, the next peeking from the right edge. Swiping is the browse; each game gets the cinematic treatment when it's on screen.

Why: maximum drama per game, natural "what's next" gesture, and the peek invites the swipe.

The day is never visible at once — you swipe to know. Browse cost is real.

M3 · THE RUNDOWN

One column, the "now" row swells

No hero/list split at all: the big games are one vertical rundown in time order, and whichever game is live/next EXPANDS inline — score and pick big — while the rest sit as one-line rows. The swollen row moves down the list as the day plays.

Why: reads like a broadcast rundown board; the whole day and the hierarchy in one shape; no dead space ever.

The hero moment is smaller than M1/M2 — less cinematic, more editorial.

M4 · THE TILE WALL

Every big game equal, at a glance

A 2-wide grid of scoreboard tiles — no hero at all. Live tiles glow green with the verdict, upcoming carry time + pick-drop, settled dim with the stamp. Tomorrow takes the last tile.

Why: the whole marquee day in one rectangle; screenshot-friendly; zero hierarchy decisions to maintain.

Nothing leads — the sweat you opened the app for is the same size as an 8 PM game.

M5 · SIDE RAIL, QUIETED (current, fixed)

Keep the two panes, fix the volume

The current idea done properly: rail shrinks to whisper-level (smaller, dimmer, tighter, narrower), the hero gets bigger type and a real pick chip, and a hairline gold rank ties the rail to the day's order. The hero unmistakably leads.
