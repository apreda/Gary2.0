Home countdown — round 2: three ownable ideas

Round 1 was too safe — a bolted-on line, a recolor of the All-Star takeover, a stories-row every app has. This round is three genuinely different metaphors for "time until Gary's next call," each one a real design idea on its own rather than a rearrangement of existing screens. Same real data throughout: Tue Jul 14, today 10–5 · +$375 · BOS ML ✓, next up Argentina @ England, 3:00 PM, live now Spain @ France. All three countdowns actually tick — open the file to watch them run.
A · THE DEPARTURE BOARD

Gary's board as a Solari flip display

The countdown becomes a physical object — split-flap tiles, like an airport or train departure board — sitting above a manifest of the week using real travel-status words: the live game is "EN ROUTE," the next one is "BOARDING," everything further out is "SCHEDULED." It's a metaphor nobody else in sports betting is using: you're not staring at a clock, you're checking when your next flight leaves.

Why it wins
  • The countdown is the hero — a crafted object, not a UI label — and the manifest format scales to any number of games without ever needing a new layout.
  • "Boarding" / "en route" / "scheduled" gives Gary a voice even in a data list.
Watch out: the flip tiles need real craft (proper tile shadow, seam, maybe an actual flip animation on change) or they read as a cheap gimmick instead of a designed one.

B · THE GOLD WATCH

Check Gary's watch

The countdown becomes a luxury wristwatch face — brushed gold bezel, hour ticks, a sweeping second hand — with the digits sitting where the watch face would be. Instead of borrowing sports-broadcast language, this leans straight into the identity you already have: gold, cigar, high-roller confidence. This is the character checking his watch, not an app showing a timer.

Why it wins
  • Most "on-brand" of the three — the gold IS the character here, not just the accent color.
  • A single strong image that could anchor a share card or App Store shot too, not just Home.
Watch out: most animation-dependent of the three. A static screenshot sells it far less than seeing the hand actually sweep — needs to be judged live, not as a still.

C · THE JUMBOTRON

Countdown as stadium scoreboard

LED-style digits with an amber glow, a riveted metal frame, team codes blocked out the way they'd sit on the arena screen above the field. This is the most "sports," least "fintech" of the three — it looks like something hanging above a stadium, not something in a trading app.

Why it wins
  • Instantly reads as sports, reinforcing "the fan's app" instead of any hint of an EV/CLV tool.
  • Pairs naturally with live state — a live game could show a running score right on the same board.
Watch out: LED/glow effects tip into kitsch fast. One glow color, no rainbow, or it reads arcade instead of premium.
