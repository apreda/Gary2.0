## Vocabulary — say / never
- "every result public" / "on his record" — never **"graded"** (our system-speak).
- "the read" / "his call" / "the reasoning" — never "AI-generated pick" or "our model/algorithm" in
  Gary-voiced copy. The NAME carries "A.I."; Gary is "he."
- "a pick for every game" — never "daily best bets" (tout language) or "comprehensive coverage" (vague).
- Never: lock, guaranteed, free money, tail me, value play, +EV, invented numbers, win-rate claims in
  ads, fake wager claims. Always 21+.

# PART B — INTERNAL ANALYSIS (never published verbatim)

## Who it's for
The betting fan: bets real money most weeks, wants a sharper read on tonight without an hour of
research, and someone to sweat it with. Not the CLV sharp; not the "guaranteed locks" chaser.

## Why plain facts win in this market
The category runs on unverifiable claims — touts who delete losses, black-box confidence scores,
parlay generators. Credibility is the scarce asset. That's WHY stating checkable facts plainly is the
strongest possible copy — the reader has been burned by hype, so hype-free IS the differentiation.
(This dynamic shapes what we say; we never perform it in copy.)

## The three differentiators (each maps to a real mechanism)
1. **The work.** Every pick carries the actual read on tonight's matchup — the starter's last five
   outings, the bullpen that threw yesterday, the 4pm lineup change. Games and props. (Mechanism: the
   research-then-decide agent loop; the constitution bans canned if-then conclusions — it's a real read,
   not pattern matching.)
2. **Every game, honestly priced.** A call on every game on the board, never a cherry-picked list, and
   he takes the underdog when the read says so. (Mechanism: the locked pick-every-game coverage policy;
   the moneyline-is-a-price framing.)
3. **The record.** Posted before the game, result shown after, wins and losses, $100 flat running total,
   never edited. (Mechanism: the in-app results tape, the X verdicts, the pinned season ledger.)

Plus the character: Gary's voice, the sweat, the owned losses — the retention layer. Character lives in
Gary-voiced surfaces (X, video VO), not in App Store copy.

## What people hire Gary for
1. "Tell me who you like tonight, and why." 2. "Save me the homework." 3. "Give me someone to sweat
with." 4. "Don't lie to me about results." — Note "make me rich" is not on the list and we never
promise it.

## Proof discipline
Every published claim maps to a checkable mechanism (every game → coverage policy; reasoning → the
rationale text; record → the tape/ledger). The public losses are the strongest proof object we own:
surfaces should make the record easy to CHECK rather than talk about honesty. Show the mechanism,
never claim the virtue.
