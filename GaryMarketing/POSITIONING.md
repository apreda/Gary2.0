# Gary A.I. — Positioning & Messaging (the marketing bible)

> Jul 5 2026. The foundational doc every piece of copy hangs off — App Store, X, video, creator briefs,
> the launch film. Derived from what the product actually does (the agent loop, the coverage policy, the
> public record), not from marketing wishes. When copy and this doc disagree, fix the copy.

## 1. Who it's for (locked long ago, restated)

**The betting fan.** The person with a sportsbook app on their phone who bets because it makes the game
matter — the sweat, the story, the "I told you so." They bet real money most weeks, they lose sometimes and
know it, and what they want is a sharper read than their own and someone to sweat it with.

NOT for: the spreadsheet sharp hunting CLV (we will never be EV tooling), and not the mark hunting
"guaranteed locks" (we will never pretend to be that).

## 2. The enemy: "Trust me bro"

Everything the betting fan can follow today runs on trust-me-bro:
- **Touts** sell locks, post the wins, delete the losses.
- **"AI picks" apps** spit a confidence percentage from a black box, or worse, auto-generate parlay slop.
- **Betting media** talks games, but never puts a real pick with real reasoning on the record.

The scarce asset in this market is not picks. It is **credibility**. Nobody believes anybody.

## 3. The controlling idea

> ### He shows his work.
> You never have to trust Gary. You can check him.

Every differentiator we have is a form of showing work, which is why this is the roof of the message
house. It is also the one claim the competition structurally cannot copy: touts can't show losses without
dying, and black-box apps have no work to show.

## 4. The three pillars (and the receipts behind each)

**PILLAR 1 — THE WORK. He does real homework on tonight's game.**
Every pick comes with the actual read: which starter is averaging 3.1 innings, which bullpen threw last
night, which lineup change dropped at 4pm, which wind is blowing out. Not season-long pattern matching —
what he thinks happens TONIGHT, in this matchup. (Receipts: the agentic research loop investigates before
Gary decides; the constitution literally forbids canned if-then conclusions; every rationale is a
multi-factor argument you can check line by line. Games AND player props.)

**PILLAR 2 — THE GUTS. Every game, and the dog when he means it.**
Gary posts a call on every game on the board, favorites and dogs, not a cherry-picked "best bets" list.
The moneyline is a price to him, not a popularity contest — when the read says the +164 underdog, he takes
the +164 underdog and tells you why. (Receipts: the locked pick-every-game coverage policy; the
price-framing architecture; real underdog calls on the record.)

**PILLAR 3 — THE RECORD. Posted before. Shown after. Never edited.**
Every pick goes up before the game starts. Every result shows after, wins and losses, with a running
profit line at a flat $100 a pick. The losses stay up forever. (Receipts: the in-app results tape; the X
account quote-tweeting its own picks with the outcome; the pinned season ledger.)

**Wrapped in: THE CHARACTER.** All of it is delivered by Gary — a bear in a suit who talks like the
sharpest friend in your group chat, sweats his calls, and owns his losses like a person. People don't
follow dashboards. They follow characters. (Duolingo made an owl a celebrity; betting has no mascot with
a public record. That slot is empty.)

## 5. Positioning statement (internal)

For betting fans who are done with touts that hide losses and AI apps that spit parlays, **Gary A.I. is a
handicapper in your pocket who does the homework on every game and shows all of it** — the pick, the full
reasoning, and a public record of every result, win or lose. Unlike pick-sellers and black-box picks apps,
you never have to trust him. You can check.

## 6. The jobs people hire Gary for (what "value" means to a user)

1. **"Tell me who you like tonight and WHY."** Confidence without an hour of research. The read is the product.
2. **"Save me the homework."** He already checked the starter, the pen, the lineup, the weather, the line.
3. **"Give me someone to sweat with."** A pick you tailed is a shared sweat; Gary reacts when it cashes or dies.
4. **"Don't lie to me."** The record is checkable. That alone separates him from everything they've followed before.

Note what is NOT on this list: "make me rich." We never promise outcomes. The promise is the work, the
guts, and the record. (Also the compliance line: 21+, never "guaranteed," never fake wagers.)

## 7. Vocabulary — say this, never that

| Say | Never | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "shows every result, win or lose" / "it's on my record" | "graded" | Grading is OUR system-speak; cold viewers don't know the term |
| "the read" / "the why" / "his call" | "analysis output", "AI-generated pick" | Human words for human trust |
| "he checked the matchup / did the homework" | "our model analyzed 10,000 data points" | Data-bragging is black-box-speak; specifics beat scale |
| "every game on the board" | "daily best bets" | Best-bets is tout language; coverage is the differentiator |
| "he'll take the dog when he means it" | "value play", "+EV" | Fan language, not quant language |
| "free — today's whole card, no signup" | "freemium tier" | Say the actual offer |
| "Gary" / "he" | "the AI" / "our algorithm" (in Gary-voiced copy) | The NAME admits the A.I.; the character never does. That wink IS the brand |
| "wins and losses stay up" | "transparent & trustworthy" | Show the mechanism, don't claim the virtue |

Banned everywhere, forever: lock, guaranteed, free money, tail me, "trust the process," rocket/fire emoji
energy, any invented number.

## 9. Proof discipline (what makes all of this legal and durable)

Every claim above maps to a checkable mechanism: coverage policy → "every game"; rationale text → "the
why"; results tape + X verdicts + pinned ledger → "the record." We never claim win rates in ads, never
invent numbers, never imply guaranteed profit, and the losses are load-bearing: **the loss on the record
is the single most persuasive object we own.** A skeptic who checks one losing day and sees it still up
converts harder than any win screenshot could make them.
