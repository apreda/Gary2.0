# "The Other Side of the Bet" — Launch Film Treatment (Tier 2)

> Founder's concept (Jul 5): a Coinbase-style evolution spot — a guy meeting a bookie in a bar → Vegas
> rising → legal sportsbooks → the AI era. 45-60s brand film for launch across TikTok/Reels/Shorts/X,
> cut-downs at 15s and 30s. Produced with Veo 3.1 (ALREADY LIVE on our Gemini key — proof render:
> `gary2.0/video-pipeline/out/veo-gary-bar.mp4`, one take, ~$2) + CapCut/DaVinci assembly + Artlist score.

## The idea in one line
Betting always had two sides: you, and the guy across the table. The other side just evolved. Meet Gary.

The visual rhyme that carries the film: in every era, something slides across a surface toward camera —
cash on a bar table, chips on felt, a phone across a kitchen counter, and finally Gary's glowing gold pick
card. Same gesture, hundred years apart. (This is the Coinbase move: one motif, time-lapsed through history.)

## Structure (45s master)

**SHOT 1 — THE BACK ROOM (0:00-0:08)**
1950s. Smoky back-of-the-bar poker room, tungsten bulb over a wooden table. A hand slides folded cash
across to a bookie in a fedora; he tips his hat brim. Sepia-warm, filmic grain, shallow focus.
VO: *"It started in the back of a bar."*
> VEO PROMPT: 1950s American back-room bar at night, film noir, a man's hand slides folded dollar bills

**SHOT 2 — THE DESERT LIGHTS UP (0:08-0:16)**
Vegas, 1970s-80s. Neon canyon of the Strip blooming at dusk; cut to a sportsbook big board flipping odds,
a dealer's hand pushing a stack of chips across green felt toward camera.

> VEO PROMPT: 1970s Las Vegas Strip at dusk, neon signs igniting one by one down the street, timelapse

**SHOT 3 — IT WENT LEGIT (0:16-0:24)**
