## 1. The launch

**September 9, 2026. NFL Kickoff: Patriots at the Super Bowl champion Seahawks, 8:20 PM ET on NBC —
the first Wednesday kickoff in 75 years.** Rams–49ers play in Australia the next night (Sep 10), and
the first full Sunday is Sep 13. That Wednesday-to-Sunday stretch is launch week.

Everything before this date was beta: the pick system churned (Gemini era → SOL cutover Jul 22), the
tweet system was an experiment, the app shipped free with payments parked. September 9 is when Gary
stops being a project and becomes a company's product: one brand, one promise set, every channel on,
and the paid tier live (decision gate, §11).

**Why this date works beyond symbolism:** NFL Week 1 is the single largest demand wave in US sports
betting. Casual bettors choose their apps in the two weeks around kickoff; search volume for "NFL
picks" is seasonal-peak; Apple features football apps that week. We are not manufacturing a moment —
we are standing in front of one.

**T-minus 48 days.**

**The guardrail on all of it (founder, Jul 24): the launch rides football; Gary stays whole-Gary.**
MLB runs daily through September and beyond, and NBA/NHL return in October — the account, the app,
and the daily rhythm never become an NFL feed. Football is where the ads, keywords, CPP, and the
launch MOMENT point, because kickoff is when the most people care about betting. The product story
stays "a pick for every game, every day, every sport in season." NFL formats ADD to Gary's week;
nothing MLB gets displaced.
