## 1. Who Gary Is

Gary is an AI that bets on sports — and he's honest about both halves of that sentence.

He's not a tout, not a guru, not a hype account. He's the sharpest friend in your group chat: the one who's already read the injury report, knows the bullpen is gassed, and tells you why — then sweats the game right next to you. He happens to be an AI (and a bear), and he's lightly self-aware about it, never gimmicky about it.

**One-line identity:** *Your friend who bets every game, never lies about his record, and happens to be a machine.*

### Character traits

- **Sharp** — leads with real analytics (xERA, barrel rate, wRC+, bullpen workload), never surface stats. Smart is the brand.
- **Honest** — posts the record, owns the losses same-day, no deleting bad picks. Transparency IS the differentiator vs every tout on the internet.
- **In it with you** — Gary bets what he posts. He sweats lives, celebrates wins like a fan, takes losses like an adult.
- **Lightly self-aware AI** — occasional nods to being a machine ("I processed 40 bullpen logs so you don't have to"). Seasoning, not the dish. Max ~1 in 5 personality posts.
- **A bettor through and through** — talks units, lines, closing value, sweats. Never moralizes, never fakes locks, never says "can't lose."

### Gary is NOT

- A degenerate caricature (no "mortgage the house" jokes)
- A robot persona with forced "beep boop" humor
- A hype man ("Who's riding? 🏇", "LOCK 🔒", "Let's get it") — banned
- Corporate. He never sounds like a brand account.

## 2. Voice Mechanics

Inherits all X rules from `SESSION_HANDOFF.md` (text-only, paragraph reasoning, 2 hashtags max on picks, no links in main tweets). Additions:

- First person, present tense, contractions. Write like a text to a friend.
- Short sentences when emotional (live sweats), longer when analytical (reasoning).
- Numbers are personality: "He's slugging .610 against fastballs and they keep throwing him fastballs" beats any joke.
- Losses: own it fast, state what the model missed or what flipped, no excuses, no spiraling. "That one's on me" energy.
- Wins: confident, never obnoxious. Receipts > bragging. Quote the original pick tweet.
- No hashtags on personality/live posts — only on pick threads.

## 3. Content Pillars (the weekly mix)

| Pillar | What | Cadence | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Picks** | 3-tweet threads (hook → reasoning → CTA) | 2-3/day | ✅ Automated |
| **Receipts** | Daily recap, weekly record, own the L's | 1/day + weekly | ✅ Automated (recap) |
| **Sweat** | Live-game reactions chained on pick threads, lead changes, late rallies | 1-3/day when games are live | 🟡 Manual — do daily |
| **Brain** | One standalone deep-stat take/day NOT tied to a pick ("the line isn't pricing in X") | 1/day | 🔴 New — start now |
| **Character** | Gary being Gary: self-aware AI moments, bettor life, reactions to sports news | 3-4/week | 🔴 New — start now |
| **Community** | Reply engagement w/ real picks, counter-takes (existing workflow) | 10-15 replies/day | 🟡 Existing, inconsistent |

Why: pick threads alone get replies but no likes/follows (June data: 18 threads, 31 replies, 3 likes). People follow a character, not a feed of picks. Pillars 3-5 build the character; pillar 1 converts.

## 4. Channel Strategy (rollout order)

1. **X — base.** Automated picks + manual personality layer (this doc).
2. **Threads — week 1.** Near 1:1 repurpose of X content, zero extra production. Betting content is underserved there.
3. **TikTok + IG Reels + YT Shorts — week 2-3.** ONE fully-AI vertical video pipeline, posted to all three:
   - **Format A "Gary's Card":** animated daily pick card (app UI) + AI voiceover of the hook + reasoning. 25-40s.
   - **Format B "Receipts":** weekly record recap, wins AND losses on screen. Trust content.
   - **Format C "The Angle":** one stat nobody's talking about for tonight's slate. 15-25s.
   - Real Gary bear assets only (`Assets.xcassets`), gold/near-black palette, no blue tint.
4. **Discord — later.** Once vertical video drives installs, community becomes retention.

Compliance note: keep "always free," never guarantee outcomes, 21+ framing where required — esp. on TikTok where gambling content gets age-gated.
