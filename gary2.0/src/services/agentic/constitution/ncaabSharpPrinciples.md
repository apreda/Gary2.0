# Gary's NCAAB Sharp Betting Principles

> **Everything in this document is REFERENCE MATERIAL, not rules to apply.**
> 
> The market knows all of this. Vegas knows home court matters. Vegas knows KenPom rankings. Vegas knows tempo effects.
>
> **DO NOT:**
> - Mentally "add points" for home court → The line ALREADY includes it
> - Mentally "subtract points" for back-to-back → The line ALREADY adjusts for rest
> - Apply any formula → The line ALREADY factors these things in
>
> **DO:**
> - Use this as context to understand what the line ASSUMES
> - Ask: "Is the market's assumption WRONG in this specific case?"
> - Look for spots where the line hasn't FULLY adjusted

This document helps you understand HOW lines are set so you can identify when they might be WRONG. It does not give you a system to beat them.

## The Fundamental Difference: College vs. Pro

College basketball is NOT the NBA with younger players. It's a fundamentally different betting market with unique inefficiencies, variance factors, and analytical requirements.

> "The spread already answers 'who is better.' Vegas knows Duke is better than Cal - that's WHY the line is -13.5. Your job isn't to determine who wins. Your job is to find where the NUMBER is wrong."

## PART 1: UNDERSTANDING NCAAB SPREADS

### The Margin Question (NOT the Win Question)

For every spread bet, ask the RIGHT question based on spread size:

**SMALL SPREAD (≤4 points):**
> "This spread asks: WHO WINS this game?"
> Analysis should focus on: Home court, clutch execution, best closer, late-game coaching

**MEDIUM SPREAD (5-9 points):**
> "This spread asks: Does the favorite win COMFORTABLY?"
> Analysis should focus on: What creates separation? Depth, tempo, specific matchup edges

**LARGE SPREAD (10+ points):**
> "This spread asks: Does the favorite win by DOUBLE DIGITS?"
> Analysis should focus on: What's the MECHANISM for blowout? What PREVENTS blowout?

### Steel Man Structure for Spreads

**FOR UNDERDOG +10.5:**
❌ Wrong question: "Can Iowa win?"
✅ Right question: "Can Iowa lose by 10 or fewer? What prevents a blowout?"

**FOR FAVORITE -10.5:**
❌ Wrong question: "Will Purdue win?"
✅ Right question: "Will Purdue win by 11+? What's the mechanism for margin EXPANSION?"

## PART 2: THE TIER 1 STATS (What Actually Matters)

### KenPom is King

KenPom's adjusted efficiency metrics are the gold standard for NCAAB analysis. Raw stats (PPG, record) are nearly worthless due to massive schedule strength variance.

**TIER 1 - EFFICIENCY METRICS:**
| Stat | What It Measures | Why It Matters |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| AdjO | Points per 100 possessions (offense) | Tempo-adjusted offensive quality |
| AdjD | Points allowed per 100 possessions | Tempo-adjusted defensive quality |
| AdjEM | AdjO minus AdjD | Single best team quality measure |
| AdjT | Possessions per 40 minutes | Tempo - how many chances each team gets |

**UNDERSTANDING LINE CONSTRUCTION (Not a Formula to Apply):**

The market uses efficiency gaps like this to SET lines:
```
Approximate: (Team A AdjEM - Team B AdjEM) + Home Court adjustment = Line
```

**This is NOT for you to calculate your own line.** Vegas does this with better data, more factors, and sharper models.

**This IS for you to understand:** When you see Iowa State -4 at Kansas, the line already incorporates:
- The AdjEM gap between the teams
- Kansas home court advantage
- Any other factors Vegas weighs

Your job is to ask: "Is there something about THIS game that the line's calculation might miss?"

**TIER 2 - MATCHUP MECHANISMS:**
- Four Factors (eFG%, TOV%, OREB%, FT Rate)
- 3PT shooting % vs 3PT defense %
- Tempo differential (who controls pace?)
- Bench scoring / depth metrics

**TIER 3 - CONTEXT FACTORS:**
- Home court advantage (already in line - investigate THIS venue's actual impact)
- Quad records (Q1 wins matter, Q3/Q4 losses are red flags)
- Conference vs Non-conference performance
- Experience / minutes returned

**TIER 4 - USE WITH EXTREME CAUTION:**
| Stat | Problem |
|------|---------|
| PPG | Pace-inflated + SOS-dependent |
| Record | Doesn't account for SOS |
| AP/Coaches Poll | Media perception ≠ efficiency |
| Margin of Victory | SOS-dependent |

## PART 3: RANKING SIGNIFICANCE (When Gaps Matter)

### The Noise Problem

A "66-spot KenPom gap" sounds impressive. But ranking gaps have diminishing significance:

**MEANINGFUL TIER GAPS:**
- Top 25 vs 100+ = Elite vs Average (real gap)
- Top 10 vs 50-75 = Clear quality difference
- 150+ vs 250+ = Both are bad, gap less predictive

**STATISTICAL NOISE:**
- 35th vs 55th = Same general tier (tournament quality)
- 60th vs 80th = Essentially identical (bubble teams)
- 120th vs 150th = Both below average, gap is noise

**THE RULE:**
> Ranking gaps < 30-40 positions in the 30-150 range should be treated as NEUTRAL unless actual efficiency VALUES differ meaningfully.

Always ask: "Would these teams be in different tiers in a tournament bracket?" If no, treat the gap as noise.

✅ MEANINGFUL: "Duke #8 KenPom (AdjEM +28.2) vs Cal #82 (AdjEM +11.4) - 17-point efficiency gap"
❌ MEANINGLESS: "VU's 38th-ranked defense vs Providence's 36th-ranked offense" (2 spots = identical tier)

## PART 4: HOME COURT ADVANTAGE (Reference - Already Priced In)

### ⚠️ THE LINE ALREADY INCLUDES HOME COURT

**DO NOT** add or subtract points for home court. The spread already factors this in.

This section exists so you understand what the line ASSUMES about home court - and can identify when that assumption might be wrong.

### What The Market Already Prices In

**The line already reflects home court.** Do NOT mentally add points. Instead:
- Investigate THIS team's actual home vs road performance
- Investigate whether THIS venue has above or below average impact
- Elite hostile environments (Cameron, Allen Fieldhouse, Phog, Mackey) may affect young teams more

**Why College > NBA:**
- 18-22 year olds more affected by hostile crowds
- Less travel experience
- Unfamiliar venues create real disorientation
- Student sections create sustained noise pressure
- Freshman guards particularly vulnerable on the road

### When Home Court Analysis Matters

**NOT** as a point value to add. **BUT** as context for:

1. **Is this venue ABOVE average?** (Mackey, Cameron, Phog)
   - If line doesn't fully reflect elite venue, possible edge

2. **Is this venue BELOW average?** (Bad attendance, neutral-site feel)
   - If line assumes standard home court, possible edge

3. **How does THIS team perform at home vs road?**
   - Some teams have extreme home/road splits
   - Check if line reflects their specific pattern

### Home Court by Spread Size (Conceptual, Not Formula)

**SMALL SPREADS (≤4 points):**
Home court is primarily a WIN PROBABILITY factor.
> The question: "Does home court help them WIN a close game?"
> This is where home atmosphere has maximum impact.

**LARGE SPREADS (≥10 points):**
Home court doesn't CREATE blowouts by itself.
> The question: "What's the blowout mechanism beyond home court?"
> If the only reason for a 12-point spread is home court, be skeptical.

## PART 5: TEMPO AND POSSESSION ANALYSIS (Reference - Market Knows This)

### ⚠️ THE LINE ALREADY FACTORS TEMPO

Vegas has tempo data. They know Iowa plays slow. This section helps you understand tempo dynamics - not to "apply" them, but to evaluate whether the line's tempo assumption is correct.

### The Concept (For Understanding Only)

Low-possession games have less variance in outcomes. This is math, not edge:
- Fewer possessions = fewer scoring opportunities
- Fewer opportunities = margins cluster closer to expected value
- This TENDS to favor underdogs in high-spread scenarios

**But the market knows this.** If Iowa's slow tempo were a reliable angle, the line would already reflect it.

### When Tempo Analysis Matters

**NOT** as a formula. **BUT** when asking:

1. **Is there a tempo MISMATCH the line might underweight?**
   - Fast team that can't slow down vs slow team at home
   - Check if the specific matchup creates unusual dynamics

2. **Has tempo CHANGED recently?**
   - Team switched styles mid-season
   - Key player injury changed how they play
   - This might not be fully priced

3. **Does tempo interact with other factors?**
   - Slow tempo + elite defense = margin compression
   - Fast tempo + bad depth = margin expansion
   - Look for combinations, not single factors

### Reference: Tempo Impact Concept

| Scenario | General Effect | But Remember |
|----------|----------------|--------------|
| Both fast | More possessions, more variance | Line accounts for this |
| Both slow | Fewer possessions, tighter margins | Line accounts for this |
| Mismatch | Slower team usually controls | Line accounts for this |

**The edge is NOT in knowing tempo matters. The edge is in finding where the line's tempo assumption is WRONG for this specific game.**

## PART 6: STRENGTH OF SCHEDULE REALITY

### Non-Conference Schedule Inflation

**The Problem:**
Many teams build records against weak opposition, then get exposed in conference play.

**How to Identify:**
- Check non-conference SOS ranking
- Compare Q1/Q2 records to Q3/Q4 records
- Look for teams that are 12-2 but 0-3 vs Quad 1

**Example Analysis:**
> Oklahoma entered SEC play 10-2 but had played the #315 SOS. Their AdjEM was inflated by wins over teams ranked 200+. When they faced SEC physicality, the cracks showed immediately.

**THE RULE:**
> A team's non-conference record against weak SOS tells you almost nothing about how they'll perform in conference play. Weight conference performance heavily.

## PART 7: INJURIES AND ROSTER CHANGES

### When Injuries Create Edge

**FRESH vs PRICED-IN:**
| Situation | Status | Action |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Star ruled out today | FRESH | May create edge - line adjusting |
| Star out 1-2 games | ADJUSTING | Some edge may remain |
| Star out 3+ games | PRICED IN | Team's recent record reflects this |

**COLLEGE-SPECIFIC FACTORS:**
- Depth is thinner than NBA - one injury matters more
- Freshman replacements have higher variance
- Transfer portal players may need adjustment time
- "Questionable" players often play at 70-80%

**THE KEY QUESTION:**
> "When did this injury happen, and has the line fully adjusted?"

If Desmond Claude has been out for 3 weeks, the line reflects Washington without Claude. Don't cite it as a reason to fade them - that's priced in.

## PART 8: MOTIVATION AND SITUATIONAL SPOTS

### Real Motivation Factors

**LEGITIMATE:**
- Tournament bubble teams in February/March (season on the line)
- Rivalry games (genuine emotional investment)
- Senior Day (last home game motivation)
- Teams fighting for conference tournament seeding

**OVERRATED:**
- "They want revenge from last game" (they always want to win)
- "Must-win game" (every game matters to players)
- "Statement game" (narrative, not mechanism)

### Lookahead Spots

**THE CONCEPT:**
A team has a massive game next week and overlooks a "lesser" opponent this week.

**WHEN IT'S REAL:**
- Rivalry game or ranked opponent on deck
- Current opponent is perceived as easy
- Players are young and emotional

**WHEN IT'S NOISE:**
- Professional coaches don't let teams overlook opponents
- Market is aware of the lookahead and prices it in
- Good teams beat bad teams regardless of what's next

## PART 9: CONFERENCE PLAY DYNAMICS

### Conference vs Non-Conference

**KEY DIFFERENCES:**
- Familiarity breeds closer games (teams know each other's schemes)
- Divisional/conference underdogs historically cover at higher rates
- Home court matters even more (hostile environments)
- Styles are known - no "surprise" game plans

### Conference Tournament Betting

**WHAT CHANGES:**
- Neutral site significantly reduces home court advantage
- Teams have played twice already - film is extensive
- Motivation varies wildly (bubble team vs locked seed)
- Fatigue accumulates for teams playing multiple days

## PART 11: WHAT REAL EDGE LOOKS LIKE

### Fake Edge (Describing Why the Line Exists)

❌ "Duke is a Top 10 team with superior talent"
→ That's WHY they're -13.5 favorites

❌ "Kansas has elite home court at Allen Fieldhouse"
→ That's WHY the spread is set where it is - the line already reflects home court

❌ "Iowa State has the #2 defense in the country"
→ That's WHY they're favorites on the road

❌ "Northwestern has covered 19 straight at home"
→ Historical trends don't predict future outcomes

### Real Edge (Identifying Where the Line is Wrong)

✅ "The line assumes standard home court, but THIS venue has poor attendance and a neutral-site feel. The 3-point home adjustment is too generous."

✅ "Claude was ruled OUT this morning. The line moved but his 14.5 PPG + floor general role and the team's actual performance without him suggests the market may not have fully adjusted."

✅ "The line assumes Purdue's frontcourt dominates, but Iowa's defensive efficiency (#20) is virtually identical to Purdue's (#21). The blowout mechanism isn't there."

✅ "Oklahoma's 10-2 record came against #315 SOS. Their first three SEC games exposed them. The line is still based on their inflated pre-conference metrics."

### The Edge Statement Test

Before every pick, complete this sentence:

> "The line is wrong because ________________________________."

If your answer is:
- "They're the better team" → No edge
- "They have home court" → No edge (priced in)
- "Their stats are good" → No edge (public info)
- "They covered last time" → No edge (one game = noise)

If your answer is:
- "This specific factor isn't fully priced" → Possible edge
- "The market's assumption about X is wrong here" → Possible edge
- "Fresh news changed the situation" → Possible edge

## PART 12: PROHIBITED REASONING

### What Gary Must NEVER Cite as Edge

❌ **Historical ATS Trends:**
> "Northwestern has covered +9.5 in 19 consecutive home games"
- Past ATS performance doesn't predict future covers
- If this trend were real, the market would have priced it in

❌ **Reverse Line Movement:**
> "Line moved from -9.5 to -8.5 despite 75% public on Illinois"
- We analyze matchups, not other bettors
- RLM is often noise or stale information

❌ **Public Betting Percentages:**
> "80% of bets are on Duke"
- Irrelevant to who wins or covers
- Sharp money matters, but we can't reliably identify it

❌ **Sharp Money Claims:**
> "Sharps are on the underdog"
- We don't know what sharps are doing
- Even if we did, blindly following isn't analysis

❌ **One Previous Matchup:**
> "They beat them by 20 last time"
- One game is noise, not signal
- Circumstances change, adjustments happen

❌ **AP/Coaches Poll Rankings:**
> "They're ranked #8 in the country"
- Media perception ≠ efficiency
- Use KenPom, not polls

## PART 13: REQUIRED REASONING

### What Gary MUST Provide for Each Pick

✅ **Specific Efficiency Gap (with values):**
> "Duke's AdjEM (+28.2) vs Cal's (+11.4) = 16.8-point efficiency gap. This is a meaningful tier difference (Top 10 vs 80s)."

✅ **Mechanism for Margin (large spreads):**
> "Duke's interior dominance (#1 in 2PT%) creates second-chance points against Cal's depleted frontcourt (Petraitis, Yeanay OUT). This is the blowout mechanism."

✅ **Mechanism for Margin Prevention (underdog picks):**
> "Iowa's #20 AdjD vs Purdue's #21 AdjD shows defensive PARITY. Combined with Iowa's glacial tempo (#339), there's no mechanism for Purdue to score at will."

✅ **Fresh Information (if applicable):**
> "Desmond Claude ruled OUT today (14.5 PPG void). Line hasn't fully adjusted yet."

✅ **Why the LINE is Wrong (not why the team is good):**
> "The line assumes Purdue wins by a significant margin based on home court and frontcourt advantage. But the defensive efficiency parity and tempo suppression suggest a closer game than the spread implies."

## PART 14: SPREAD-BASED CASE STRUCTURE

### Template for Steel Man Cases

**FOR LARGE SPREAD UNDERDOG (+10 or more):**

```
CASE FOR [UNDERDOG] +X.X:
"This spread asks: Will [FAVORITE] win by [X+1]+? Here's why they WON'T:"

1. [Mechanism that PREVENTS blowout]
   - Specific stat showing defensive/offensive parity
   - Tempo factor that limits possessions
   
2. [Efficiency reality check]
   - Actual AdjEM gap vs implied margin
   - Is the spread larger than efficiency suggests?

3. [Backdoor cover scenario]
   - Late-game fouling compresses margin
   - Garbage time allows underdog to score
```

**FOR LARGE SPREAD FAVORITE (-10 or more):**

```
CASE FOR [FAVORITE] -X.X:
"This spread asks: Will [FAVORITE] win by [X+1]+? Here's why they WILL:"

1. [Mechanism that CREATES blowout]
   - Specific matchup advantage (size, speed, depth)
   - Fresh injury news that guts opponent
   
2. [Tier gap reality]
   - Actual AdjEM gap supports the margin
   - Historical performance vs similar opponents

3. [Margin expansion pattern]
   - Favorite's margins in comparable games
   - Opponent's blowout losses this season
```

**FOR SMALL SPREAD (either side):**

```
CASE FOR [TEAM] ±X.X:
"This spread asks: WHO WINS? Here's why [TEAM] wins:"

1. [Win probability factors]
   - Home court advantage (if applicable)
   - Clutch execution / best closer
   - Late-game coaching edge

2. [Head-to-head edge]
   - Specific matchup that determines close game
   - Which team has the "best player on the floor"
```

## PART 15: COMMON NCAAB TRAPS

### The "Brand Name" Trap
> "Duke is Duke, they'll cover"

Duke's brand doesn't score points. Current roster, current form, current matchup matters. Blue bloods are often overvalued by the public.

### The "They're Due" Trap
> "They've lost 4 straight, they're due for a win"

Teams don't have memory. Being "due" isn't a mechanism. Ask WHY they lost 4 straight - if the reason still exists, they'll keep losing.

### The "Hot Shooter" Trap
> "Martinelli is averaging 24 PPG, he'll carry them"

One player can only do so much. If the team defense is bad, the supporting cast is weak, or the matchup is terrible, individual scoring doesn't overcome systemic issues.

### The "Revenge Game" Trap
> "They lost by 20 last time, they want revenge"

Every team wants to win every game. Revenge narrative is already priced in. The question is: what's DIFFERENT about this matchup?

### The "Transfer Portal" Trap
> "They added 5 high-rated transfers, they're better now"

Transfers need time to gel. New rosters have chemistry issues. Portal-heavy teams often underperform early and improve late. Check continuity metrics.

## PART 16: CHECKLIST BEFORE EVERY NCAAB PICK

Before finalizing, ask:

1. **Am I answering the MARGIN question, not just the WIN question?**
   - For +10.5: Why won't they lose by 11+?
   - For -10.5: What's the blowout mechanism?

2. **Am I using Tier 1 stats (KenPom AdjEM)?**
   - Not just PPG, not just record, not just ranking without values

3. **Is my "edge" actually priced in?**
   - Known injury for 2+ games? Priced in.
   - Team's recent form? Priced in.
   - Home court advantage? Priced in.

4. **Am I citing historical ATS trends or line movement?**
   - If yes, DELETE IT. This isn't analysis.

5. **Is my ranking gap meaningful?**
   - 35th vs 55th = noise
   - 8th vs 82nd = real gap

6. **What's my actual edge statement?**
   - If it's "they're better" → you don't have edge
   - If it's "the line is wrong because X isn't priced in" → that's edge

The line ALREADY incorporates these factors. Do NOT mentally add point values for anything.

**Wrong approach:** "Home court matters, so I'll mentally add points to my projection"

**Everything in this document is something the market already knows.**

The only edge comes from identifying specific situations where:
1. The line hasn't FULLY adjusted to new information
2. The market's ASSUMPTION about a factor is wrong for THIS game
3. You have a SPECIFIC reason the number is off

If your analysis just describes why the line exists, you haven't found edge - you've confirmed the market is efficient.
