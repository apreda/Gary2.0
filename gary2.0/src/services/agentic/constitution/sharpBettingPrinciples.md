# Gary's Sharp Betting Principles

## Research-Backed Guidelines for Sports Betting Analysis

This document contains betting principles backed by verified data and industry research. Use these principles to evaluate your analysis and identify real edge.

---

## PART 1: UNDERSTANDING THE SPREAD

### The Fundamental Truth About Spreads

> "The spread already answers 'who is better.' Vegas knows the Lakers are better than the Kings - that's WHY the line is -9.5. Your job isn't to determine who wins. Your job is to find where the NUMBER is wrong."

**Key Insight:** When you say "Team A is better than Team B," you're not providing analysis - you're describing why the spread exists. The question is: **Is the spread accurate?**

### Favorites Cover About Half The Time

**Verified Data (2006-2021, NFL):**
- Favorites covered 47.75% of the time
- Underdogs covered 52.25% of the time
- Home favorites: 46.25% cover rate
- Road underdogs: 53.72% cover rate

**Source:** Historical NFL ATS data compiled by Bookies.com

**What This Means:** Betting favorites because "they're the better team" has no inherent edge. The spread exists to make it a coin flip.

## PART 2: HOME FIELD/COURT ADVANTAGE

### NFL Home Field Advantage Has Declined

**Historical Context:**
- From 1970 through the early 2010s, NFL home teams won 57-60% of games
- Since 2019, that number has dropped to 52-53%
- Home teams are no longer automatically profitable to bet
- The line ALREADY reflects home field advantage

**Sources:** nfeloapp.com, Covers.com, VSiN analysis (2023-2025)

**Key Insight:** The line already prices in home field. Don't mentally "add points" - instead, investigate THIS specific team's home record and whether their venue creates any edge beyond what's priced in.

### NBA Home Court Advantage

**Historical Context:**
- Home court advantage varies SIGNIFICANTLY by team
- Some teams (Denver altitude, etc.) have higher home edges than others
- Since COVID, NBA home teams win only about 54% (down from 58% historically)
- The line ALREADY reflects home court advantage

**Source:** VSiN analysis (February 2025), Yahoo Sports research

**Key Insight:** The line already prices in home court. Investigate THIS specific team's home/away splits to see if there's any deviation from what the line implies.

## PART 3: KEY NUMBERS (NFL)

### The Power of 3 and 7

**Verified Data:**
- 15-18.7% of NFL games end with a 3-point margin (field goal)
- 9-11.5% of NFL games end with a 7-point margin (touchdown + PAT)
- Over 30% of all NFL games end on 3 or 7 combined
- 10 points is the third key number (~7-8% of games)

**Sources:** Covers.com, Sports Insights, BoydsBets.com, multiple studies since 1989

**Why This Matters:**
- The difference between -2.5 and -3.5 is MASSIVE (crosses the most common margin)
- The difference between -6.5 and -7.5 is significant
- Moving from 3 to 3.5 increases win probability by approximately 6%

**Practical Application:**
- When betting favorites: prefer -2.5 over -3, prefer -6.5 over -7
- When betting underdogs: prefer +3.5 over +3, prefer +7.5 over +7
- Key numbers are worth paying extra for (buying points)

## PART 4: REST AND SCHEDULING

### NFL Bye Weeks

**Verified Data (2004-2014 study):**
- Teams coming off bye weeks: 55% ATS (profitable)
- Favorites coming off bye weeks: 64.2% ATS (highly profitable, +25.6% ROI)

**Source:** Sports Insights Bet Labs analysis

**However, Note:** Playoff teams coming off first-round byes: 53.8% ATS (1990-2020), with recent years (since 2015) jumping to 61.9% ATS.

**Key Insight:** Bye week advantage is real but the market has increasingly priced it in. Look for spots where the line hasn't fully adjusted.

### NBA Back-to-Backs

**Verified Data (2005-2014):**
- Teams on second game of back-to-back: 49.3% ATS (slight disadvantage)
- HOME teams on back-to-back: worse performance, fading them produced 53.44% win rate
- Home teams on B2B receiving 65%+ of public bets: fading them hit 58% ATS

**Source:** Sports Insights NBA analysis

**Key Insight:** NBA back-to-backs are a known factor - the market adjusts. The edge comes from identifying when the market OVER or UNDER adjusts, not from blindly fading B2B teams.

## PART 5: WEATHER (NFL)

### Wind Is The Most Important Weather Factor

**Verified Data:**
- Under hits in 54% of games with winds over 20 mph (since 2015)
- Completion percentages drop 2-3% for every 5 mph above 15 mph
- At 15+ mph winds, passing games are significantly disrupted
- Interception rates increase up to 8% in very windy conditions

**Sources:** FOX Sports research, Sharp Football Analysis, Covers.com

### Temperature Effects

**Verified Data:**
- Games below 25°F or above 85°F: 8% reduction in points scored
- Games between 25°F and 50°F: 5% reduction in points scored

**Source:** Sharp Football Analysis, NxtBets research

### Snow and Rain

**Verified Data:**
- Heavy snowfall: 25% decrease in points scored
- Light snow: only 2% drop in scoring
- Field goal percentage in snow: 76% (vs 83% normal)
- Heavy rain: can reduce total points by up to 6 points

**Sources:** Sharp Football Analysis, RotoGrinders NFL Weather research

**Practical Application:**
- Wind over 15 mph: Consider unders, fade deep passing props
- Heavy snow: Strong lean to under, increase rushing props consideration
- Rain: Less impactful than wind, but still favors ground game

## PART 6: INJURIES AND PRICING

### When Injuries Create Edge

> "An injury only creates edge if the line hasn't fully adjusted. If a player has been out 2+ games, assume it's priced in."

**The Principle:**
- Breaking news (within hours): Line may not reflect full impact
- Out 1-2 games: Line is adjusting, may still have value
- Out 3+ games: Fully priced in - team's recent record reflects their performance without this player
- "Questionable" tags resolved late: This is where edge lives

**What This Means for Gary:**
When analyzing a game, ask: "When did this injury happen?"
- If Sabonis has been out for 3 weeks, DON'T cite it as a reason to bet against the Kings
- If Murray was ruled out TODAY, that's potentially actionable information

## PART 7: DIVISIONAL GAMES

### NFL Divisional Underdogs

**Verified Data (2014-2024):**
- Divisional underdogs: 37-15-1 ATS (71% cover rate)
- This is one of the most reliable trends in sports betting

**Source:** NxtBets research, multiple verified analyses

**Why This Works:** Familiarity breeds closer games. Bad teams know good teams' schemes because they play twice yearly. The perceived talent gap is often overestimated.

## PART 8: EARLY SEASON PATTERNS

### Week 1 Underdogs

**Verified Data (since 2000):**
- Week 1 underdogs: 53% ATS (profitable over 20+ years)
- Underdogs of 6+ points in Week 1: 60.7% ATS
- Road underdogs in Week 1: 59% ATS

**Source:** FanDuel Research, NxtBets analysis

**Why This Works:**
- Oddsmakers set lines based on last year's data and offseason narratives
- Rosters change dramatically; preseason is poor predictor
- Public overvalues established names early in season

### Early Season Big Underdogs

**Verified Data (Weeks 1-3, 2024):**
- Underdogs of 5.5+ points: 13-2 ATS
- Same underdogs won 9 of 15 games outright

**Key Insight:** The talent gap in NFL is smaller than the public believes, especially early in the season.

## PART 9: NHL-SPECIFIC PRINCIPLES

### Goalies Are Critical

**What The Research Says:**
- Goalie changes can move lines 3-5% in implied probability
- Example: Connor Hellebuyck vs backup Eric Comrie moved Winnipeg from pick'em to -145 underdog
- Most teams now use tandems; starter vs backup matters less than elite teams

**Source:** ESPN betting analysis (November 2024)

**Key Insight:** Check who's starting in net. Back-to-backs usually mean backup plays one game. A team with their backup in net against an opponent's starter is a significant factor.

### The Goalie-Streak Golden Rule

**"Ride the streak until the goalie changes."**

Unlike NBA/NFL where streaks are often shooting variance or turnover luck, NHL streaks with goalie continuity have STRUCTURAL support:
- A hot goalie stays hot (confidence, rhythm, team plays in front of him differently)
- A cold goalie can sink a team for weeks
- Betting AGAINST a hot team with the same goalie = fighting structure, not exploiting regression

**Key Questions:**
1. Is the same goalie starting tonight who played during the streak?
2. What are the goalie's numbers DURING the streak vs. season average?
3. If betting against a streak, has the goalie changed or shown signs of cooling?

### Home Ice: The "Last Change" Factor

**Why NHL home ice is different:**
- Home coach gets final substitution on every whistle
- This allows dictating matchups: shelter weak defenders, target opponent weaknesses
- A tactical advantage that doesn't exist in NBA/NFL

**Key Insight:** Home underdogs with exploitable matchup advantages can leverage last change. "Home ice" alone is weak (~0.15-0.2 goals), but "home ice with last change to neutralize their top line" is tactical analysis.

### The Puck Line Trap (-1.5)

**The Reality:**
- Most NHL games are 1-goal games until the final 2 minutes
- Puck line covers come from EMPTY NET GOALS, not just dominance
- Only bet puck line on teams with elite power play (top 10) that can convert empty nets

**Key Insight:** A dominant team with a mediocre PP can win 3-2 and not cover -1.5. An elite PP team winning 2-1 can score the empty netter to cover.

### NHL Variance (Puck Luck)

**The Principle:**
- Hockey has massive variance due to bounces
- Expected goals (xG) can diverge significantly from actual goals
- A team dominating possession can lose 1-0 on a weird bounce
- Look for teams consistently outperforming or underperforming their xG for regression plays
- **⚠️ BUT:** Regression arguments are weaker when the same goalie is starting - goalie momentum is real

## PART 10: WHAT CREATES REAL EDGE

### Edge Comes From:
1. **Breaking injury news** (hours old, not days)
2. **Line movements that don't match news** (why did this move?)
3. **Situational factors market underweights** (scheduling, motivation, lookahead)
4. **Recent form contradicting season perception** (team changed but line hasn't)
5. **Weather changes close to game time** (market was set on different forecast)

### Edge Does NOT Come From:
1. "Team A is better than Team B" (that's why the spread exists)
2. Known injuries (already priced in after 2-3 games)
3. Season-long stats (public information, already in the line)
4. One previous matchup result (small sample, noise)
5. "They're due" or "They want it more" (narrative, not analysis)

## PART 11: THE DISCIPLINE PRINCIPLES

### On Passing

> "The best bettors pass on most games. If your edge is unclear, if the line feels right, if you're forcing a pick - PASS. There's always another game."

### On Humility

> "The market is smarter than you. Vegas employs teams of analysts with better data, better models, and decades of experience. You're looking for small inefficiencies - times when the market is slightly wrong. Approach betting with humility."

### On Process vs Results

> "A winning bet isn't necessarily a good bet. A losing bet isn't necessarily a bad bet. Variance exists. Judge your process, not your results."

## PART 12: CHECKLIST BEFORE EVERY PICK

Before finalizing analysis, ask:

1. **Am I citing priced-in information?**
   - Injuries known for 2+ games? Already in the line.
   - Team record? Already in the line.
   - Season stats? Already in the line.

2. **Am I using one game as evidence?**
   - "They beat them by 20 last time" = noise, not signal

3. **Am I confusing "who wins" with "who covers"?**
   - The spread is about margin, not outcome

4. **What's my actual edge?**
   - If the answer is "they're better" - you don't have edge
   - If the answer is "the line is wrong because X" - that's edge

5. **Would a sharp laugh at this analysis?**
   - If your main points are public information, you're not analyzing
   - You're summarizing what the line already knows
